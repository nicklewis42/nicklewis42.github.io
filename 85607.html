Air Fried Chips

The Air Fryer makes great chips although it does take a bit more time and effort.

Don't skimp on the cooking time, they should be well browned and crispy.

  1. Set the fryer to 180 C.
  2. Peel the potatoes and cut roughly into chips, aim for lots of angles rather than neatness.
  3. Soak the chips for ten minutes in colde water.
  4. Drain and remove excess water with Kitchen roll.
  5. Toss the chips in a little oil, they should be coated in oil.
  6. Air fry the chips turning them every five minutes, they should be done in 20 to 30 minutes.
Air Fried Jacket Potatoes

Potatoes - You want a size so two or three will fit the Fryer.

Oil and salt for rubbing.

  1. Set the fryer to 200 C.
  2. Thoroughly clean the potatoes.
  3. Rub the skins in the salt and oil.
  4. Put the potatoes in the fryer for 30 minutes.
  5. Turn the spuds and cook for a further 20 minutes.
Air fried sausages

The air fryer makes great sausages.

  1. Set the air fryer to 180 C.
  2. Cook the sausages for 10 minutes.
  3. Turn and cook for a further 5 minutes.
  4. Rest for a few minutes, they will be very hot.

Baked Rice Pudding

Basic rice pudding can be zinged up with lemon zest,sultanas, cinnamon etc....

Try with a teaspoon of mixed spice and a teaspoon of cinnamon added to the liquid at the start and a handful of dried fruit mixed in later.

Milk600ml
Double Cream300ml
caster sugar25g
Butter25g
Pudding rice100g
  1. Pre heat oven to gas mark 1 (140C)
  2. Put the milk and cream into a suacepan, bring to a simmer
  3. Stir in the sugar and rice
  4. Transfer mixture to a shallow ovenproof dish and dot the butter on top. Bake for 30 mins, then stir well and cook for 1 hr more until the pudding is soft and creamy, and a golden skin has formed on top.
  5. Once cooked, rest for 10 mins before serving.

The depth and type of dish you use will affect the cooking time, so if the pudding seems too loose, return to the oven and check every 10 mins or so.

Fritata

A kind of caseless quiche cooked in a frying pan. Like a quiche the fillings can be pretty much anything although the peas and potatoes are traditional ingedients. Fillings should all be cooked before the eggs are added. Quantites should work in a medium sized pan and serve three or four. Note you need an oven proof frying pan for this.

Eggs6ea
Cream150ml
Grated Cheese Strong cheddar is good but whatever works for you.
Potatoes500g
Peas optional
Bacon optional
Sausages optional
Mushrooms optional
Milk As required
  1. Peel the potatoes and cut into half inch cubes.
  2. Other than the eggs cream and cheese everything needs to be cooked before the eggs are added so boil/steam the potatoes and peas, bacon veg etc. can be precooked or sauted in the pan before adding the eggs.
  3. At about this point you should be putting the oven on at Gas 4, you don't wnat to put the pan into a cold oven.
  4. Add everything except the cheese, eggs and cream to the hot pan, anything that needs cooking should go in first, fragile things (like peas) last.
  5. While everything is still hot distribute the cooked meats and some chopped or grated cheese in the pan.
  6. Beat the cream and eggs together and pour into the hot pan.
  7. Heat on the hob until starting to cook round the edges then the pan goes into the preheated (Gas 4) oven.
  8. Cook for about thirty minutes then turn over onto a plate slice and serve. DO NOT FORGET THE PAN HANDLE IS HOT!!!

Rice (Absorption method)

This is how you cook white rice by the absorption method.

Dried rice75gper person
Water Twice the rice by volume
  1. Wash the rice thoroughy in a sieve under the cold tap.
  2. Put the rice and water in a saucepan and bring to a good strong boil.
  3. As soon as the rice is boiling well give it one quick stir and turn the gas down low, put on a tight fitting lid and leave well alone for 10 minutes exactly.

Risotto base

This recipe is for the base add peas, chicken, mushrooms etc. etc... About enough for 2 or 3.

If you want to do this in a slow cooker use 150g rice to 600g stock and cook on high, it should take about an hour and a half.

Onions A single onion is great or a few shallots
Risotto rice170g
Chicken stock1000mlVegetable stock if you want a veggie version
Butter A couple of knobs
Oil 
Finely grated cheese To finish
  1. Prepare the stock and bring it to the boil then leave the pan on a very low heat.
  2. Finely chop the onions.
  3. Melt the first knob of butter in a saucepan with a splash of oil and add the onions.
  4. Add the rice and continue to fry, the rice should "crack" but do not allow it to burn.
  5. Now add the wine, it should boil but will cool the pan.
  6. Now patience is required, keep gently on the simmer, watch it like a hawk and stir very often. Keep adding the stock a ladel at a time each time it is absorbed.

  8. Add the second knob of butter and stir, as soon as this has been incorporated it's ready to serve.
  9. Serve and scatter the cheese on top, for the purist this will be parmesan.

Roast Pork

For a Loin Joint

  1. 35 mins per 500g plus 35 mins at Gas 5. Pork has a high enough fat content so that it does not usually require basting.
Roast Potatoes
Plain flour1Little
Potatoes As required
Oil / Fat Whatever your taste
  1. Put a roasting tin in the oven (one big enough to take the potatoes in a single layer) and heat oven to gas 7.
  2. Peel the potatoes and cut each into 4 even-sized pieces if they are medium size, 2-3 if smaller (5cm pieces).
  3. Drop the potatoes into a large pan and pour in enough water to barely cover them. Add salt, then wait for the water to boil. As soon as the water reaches a full rolling boil, lower the heat, put your timer on and simmer the potatoes uncovered, reasonably vigorously, for 5 mins.
  4. Meanwhile, put your choice of fat into the hot roasting tin and heat it in the oven for a few minutes, so it is really hot.
  5. Drain the potatoes in a colander. Now it is time to rough them up a bit, shake the colander back and forth a few times to fluff up the outsides. Sprinkle with the flour, and give another shake or two so they are evenly and thinly coated.
  6. Carefully put the potatoes into the hot fat, they will sizzle as they go in, then turn and roll them around so they are coated all over. Spread them in a single layer making sure they have plenty of room.
  7. Roast the potatoes for 15 mins, then take them out of the oven and turn them over. Roast for another 15 mins and turn them over again. Put them back in the oven for another 10-20 mins, or however long it takes to get them really golden and crisp. The colouring will be uneven, which is what you want. Scatter with salt and serve straight away.

Roasting time varies with variety and age but don't cut it fine, budget for about 75 minutes.

Slow cooker beef joint

This is not about a one pot meal but is about a perfect joint. Serve with whatever additional veg you want.

beef joint650gTopside is probably the best inexpensive joint for this recipe. Brisket is nice but won't slice well, don't be tempted to try it.
Onion1A medium sized onion, you don't need a big one.
beef stock500ml
Red wine1
Corn flour6dstsp
  1. Roughly chop the carrots slice the onions and drop into the slow cooker.
  2. Thoroughly sear the joint all over to develop the flavour.
  3. Add the joint to the slow cooker.
  4. Mix the stock and red wine, slake and then add 3 tbsp of the corn flour.
  5. Pour the stock over the joint, it doesn't need to be completely covered but should be mostly covered.

  9. Serve with roasties, yorkshires and veg.

This is a great way to get a small joint well done without drying it out.

Classic beef stew, it only gets better the longer you cook it!

This is a way to cook a (small) whole chicken in the slow cooker, you can use the result just like a roast chicken but it will be more moist and tender. Bear in mind if you are one who likes to eat the skin it'll be very pale and bland.

Chicken1eaA whole chicken of a size to fit comfortably in your slow cooker
Onion1A medium sized onion, you don't need a big one.
  1. Slice the onion into rings and jumble them into the slow cooker.
  2. Put the chicken on top and add 100ml or so of water
  3. Cook on low until done, experience says 4 hours but the interior should reach 70C juices run clear and so on.

Victoria Sponge

You can go by the weights below but you will get more consistent results by weighing the eggs in their shells and use the same weight of caster sugar, margarine and flour.

Caster sugar8oz
Margarine8oz
SR flour8oz
Eggs4each
Milk a little
Butter or margarine100g
Icing sugar200g
  1. Beat Marg and caster sugar together.
  2. Mix in a little of the flour and then add the eggs.
  3. Mix in the remaining flour.
  4. Add enough milk to give a soft dropping consistency.
  5. Divide between two lined sandwich tins.
  6. Bake at gas 3.5 for 38 minutes.
  7. Fill with butter cream made by blending the icing sugar into the margarine.

The key to getting a light well risen sponge is the beating of the fat and sugar together at the start. After that everything should be done gently to avoid driving out the air.

If you prefer to use a mechanical mixer you can put all the ingredients in and mix in one step, the power of the mixer will ensure the mix is aerated.

  1. Melt the butter in a saucepan and whisk in the flour and mustard powder. Cook for a minute or two, then gradually add the stout and the Worcestershire sauce, stirring constantly until smooth. Bring to the boil and immediately reduce the heat.
