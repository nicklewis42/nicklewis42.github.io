Slow cooker beef joint

Introduction

This is not about a one pot meal but is about a perfect joint. Serve with whatever additional veg you want.

DescriptionQuantityUOMComment
beef joint650gTopside is probably the best inexpensive joint for this recipe. Brisket is nice but won't slice well, don't be tempted to try it.
Onion1A medium sized onion, you don't need a big one.
beef stock500ml
Red wine1
Corn flour6dstsp

Method

Roughly chop the carrots slice the onions and drop into the slow cooker.

Thoroughly sear the joint all over to develop the flavour.

Add the joint to the slow cooker.

Mix the stock and red wine, slake and then add 3 tbsp of the corn flour.

Pour the stock over the joint, it doesn't need to be completely covered but should be mostly covered.

Cook on low until the centre reaches 50C for rare or 70 for well done, this should take 3 to 5 hours for a good sized joint 90-120 minutes for a very small one.

Take the beef out and wrap in foil to rest.

While the beef is resting transfer most of the liquid to a saucepan, reduce and thicken with remaining cornflour.

Serve with roasties, yorkshires and veg.

Final Comments

This is a great way to get a small joint well done without drying it out.
